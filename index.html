Understanding Bipolar Disorder

Types of Bipolar Disorder

üî¥ Bipolar I

Defined by manic episodes lasting at least 7 days (or severe enough to require hospitalization). Depressive episodes typically last 2+ weeks.

  • Full manic episodes (extreme highs)
  • May include psychotic features
  • Depressive episodes common but not required for diagnosis
  • About 1% of population

üü† Bipolar II

Characterized by hypomanic episodes (less severe than mania) and major depressive episodes. Often misdiagnosed as depression.

  • Hypomanic episodes (elevated but functional)
  • More time spent depressed than hypomanic
  • No full manic episodes
  • About 0.4% of population

üü° Cyclothymia

Chronic fluctuating mood involving periods of hypomanic and depressive symptoms that don't meet criteria for full episodes.

  • Symptoms for 2+ years
  • Less severe but persistent
  • Can progress to Bipolar I or II
  • About 0.4-1% of population

Recognizing Episodes

‚¨ÜÔ∏è Manic/Hypomanic Episodes

  • Abnormally elevated or irritable mood
  • Decreased need for sleep (feeling rested after 3 hours)
  • Racing thoughts, rapid speech
  • Increased goal-directed activity or agitation
  • Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity
  • Risky behavior (spending, sex, investments)
  • Easily distracted

‚¨áÔ∏è Depressive Episodes

  • Persistent sad, empty, or hopeless mood
  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
  • Significant weight/appetite changes
  • Sleeping too much or too little
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Treatment Approaches

üè• Mood Stabilizers

Lithium, valproate, and carbamazepine help prevent manic and depressive episodes. Often first-line treatment.

üíä Atypical Antipsychotics

Quetiapine, aripiprazole, olanzapine can treat acute episodes and provide maintenance. Often combined with mood stabilizers.

üí¨ Psychotherapy

CBT, interpersonal therapy, and family-focused therapy help with coping, relationships, and recognizing early warning signs.

üåô Lifestyle Management

Regular sleep schedule, stress management, avoiding alcohol/drugs, and routine are crucial for stability.
